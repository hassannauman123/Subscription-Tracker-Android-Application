Vision Statement

In a world with ever-increasing subscription services, Track-It is a powerful organizational tool to assist consumers with tracking and managing their subscriptions.


What is Track-it?

Track-It will allow users to add, search, edit and organize every service they subscribe to along with the subscription information and details. The app will allow users to create a user account so that any information they enter is securely saved and retrievable from multiple devices. The user will be able to see the total costs involved from all of the services they are subscribed to. This information can be displayed in the form of daily, monthly and yearly total costs from all the subscriptions that they have. This feature will help the users get a firm grasp of all their recurring costs and allow them to more efficiently and effectively create and stick to a budget. The ability to visualize all subscriptions will also help users to decide if some services are not being utilized enough to justify the cost.


Who is Track-it for?

Track-It is primarily designed for consumers who have multiple paid subscriptions. It is also intended for those that are budget conscious and want an easier way to track their recurring expenses.


Track-it's value

Many consumers have multiple subscriptions, membership fees and other recurring payments like Amazon Prime, Netflix, Costco, gym memberships, parking lot permits, etc. It is easy to forget about all of these recurring subscriptions, and to not be fully aware of all of their costs. Track-It will help them remember those subscriptions by combining all of that information into one place, allowing users to see a detailed overview of all of their recurring expenses and manage their subscriptions in one convenient app.


How our success will be measured

The subscription Tracker project will be considered a success if at least 40% of a specific target user (consumers with at least 4 paid subscriptions) who start using the system continue to use the system after 1 year, or we are able to generate $500,000+ from ad revenue per year.

Postmortem

What went right?

We successfully delivered a fully functioning app, including a functional database and adequate testing. We also did a great job at identifying key features for the app right at the start of the project. Only minor changes were made to the features we were wanting to implement.


What went wrong?

There was a clearly uneven workload distribution amongst the group members. This problem was identified and vocalized at the end of iteration 2. After these concerns were brought to light, we did find that overall teamwork, workload distribution and communication improved. We also attempted to correct this by having group members assign themselves to an issue they are working on to avoid multiple people working on the same thing at once and to improve workload distribution.


What did you learn about project development?

Communication is key. Had communication been the focus we likely could have accomplished a lot more than what we did, especially given we are a group of 6. Unfortunately, the importance of communication was overlooked for the majority of the project. Also scheduling time to meet up as a group should have occured on a more routine basis.


How did the project change from the initial iteration?

We opted to eliminate the features that involved advertisements and components external to the app. These included the feature that would display advertisements and other subscriptions a user may be interested in based on services the user is already subscribed to. We felt these features would be excessively complex without adding enough value to justify the time required to implement them, give that the core functionality of the app doesn't involve these features. We opted to instead focus on the main features that provide the functionality of the app, and will introduce advertisements once we establish a solid user base.
